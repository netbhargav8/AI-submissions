Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Before writing machine learning code, we analyze structural patterns. For the Titanic dataset, this means figuring out how demographic traits (like gender, age, and passenger class) influenced survival rates.

Feature Engineering: Raw data isn't always model-ready. We transform messy variables into meaningful ones. For example, instead of tracking raw numbers for SibSp (siblings/spouses) and Parch (parents/children) separately, we combine them to engineer a single column: FamilySize.

Model Duel (Linear vs. Ensemble):

Logistic Regression: A linear model that assigns a strict weight coefficient to each feature. It works best when relationships are straightforward and additive.

Random Forest: An ensemble model made of hundreds of unique Decision Trees that vote on the final outcome. It handles complex, non-linear human interactions effortlessly (e.g., "If Passenger Class is 3 AND gender is male AND age is under 10...").
